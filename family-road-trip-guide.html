Car Entertainment Guide

Keep the kids happy for ~17 hours of driving across 2 days

The Golden Rule: Rotate Every 30-45 Minutes

Pack a "surprise bin" accessible from the front seat. Swap activities every 30-45 minutes to keep things fresh. Novelty is more powerful than any single toy — even dollar store surprises wrapped in tissue paper can buy you an hour of peace.

Screen-Free Activities

Melissa & Doug Water Wow Pads

Ages 4 & 8

Reusable water-based painting pads — fill the pen with water, paint the pages, let them dry, and do it again. Zero mess. These are absolute road trip gold.

Magnetic Drawing Boards & Activities

Ages 4 & 8

Magna Doodle-style drawing boards or magnetic tangram puzzles. The 8-year-old might enjoy magnetic building tiles. Nothing rolls under seats!

Sticker Books & Window Clings

Age 4 Age 8

Reusable sticker scenes for the 4-year-old. The 8-year-old might prefer sticker-by-number books (more challenging). Window clings from the dollar store let them decorate their car window.

New Library Books

Ages 4 & 8

Check out a stack of new-to-them books from the library before the trip. The novelty factor makes these way more engaging than books they already own. Graphic novels and "I Spy" books are great for the 8-year-old.

Coloring & Activity Books

Age 4 Age 8

Chunky crayons and simple coloring books for the 4-year-old. The 8-year-old might enjoy crossword puzzles, word searches, or Mad Libs (Junior edition). Use a clipboard or lap desk for a stable surface.

Small Toys & Figurines

Ages 4 & 8

A small bag of action figures, toy cars, or Polly Pockets. Bonus: wrap a few in tissue paper as "surprise gifts" to open at scheduled intervals. Dollar store toys work perfectly for this.

Audio Entertainment

Audiobooks (Play Through Car Speakers)

Ages 4 & 8

Download from your library's Libby or Hoopla app — free with a library card! Great picks that work for both ages:

• The Magic Tree House series — perfect for the 8-year-old, engaging enough for the 4-year-old

• Winnie the Pooh — timeless, gentle, both ages love it

• Dog Man series — funny and engaging for the 8-year-old

• Frog and Toad — short chapters, sweet stories, great for the 4-year-old

Kid-Friendly Podcasts

Ages 4 & 8

Download episodes before the trip (cell service may be spotty in Ontario):

• Wow in the World — science and wonder, great for curious kids

• Story Pirates — stories written by kids, performed by comedians

• But Why? — answers kids' real questions about the world

Music & Sing-Alongs

Ages 4 & 8

Create a family road trip playlist before you leave! Mix their favorites with road trip classics. Freeze dance (everyone freezes when you pause the music) works even in car seats.

Screen Time (Strategic Use)

Tablets — The Secret Weapon

Ages 4 & 8

Download content before the trip — you'll lose cell service in parts of Ontario and rural New York.

• Movies: Download 3-4 movies each on their tablets. Save the favorites for the longest/hardest stretches.

• Apps: Khan Academy Kids (age 4), Toca Boca apps (both), PBS Kids Games (age 4), Minecraft (age 8)

• Key tip: Get volume-limiting headphones for each kid. Your sanity will thank you.

Pro tip: Save screen time as your "ace in the hole" for the hardest parts of the drive — late afternoon on Day 1, or the last 2 hours before Boston.

Car Games (No Supplies Needed)

Games the Whole Family Can Play

Ages 4 & 8

• I Spy — Classic. The 4-year-old can play with colors ("I spy something blue").

• License Plate Game — Spot plates from different states/provinces. The 8-year-old can keep a written tally.

• 20 Questions — Great for the 8-year-old. Simplify for the 4-year-old (animal, food, or toy?).

• Would You Rather? — Silly options keep everyone laughing. "Would you rather have spaghetti hair or pizza ears?"

• The Alphabet Game — Find letters A-Z on signs, license plates, and billboards in order.

• Scavenger Hunt — Make a simple picture list before the trip (red barn, cow, bridge, police car, etc.).

Suggested Activity Rotation Schedule

First hour: Excitement carries them — books, window watching, car games
Hour 2: Activity bin — Water Wow, stickers, coloring
Hour 3: Audiobook or podcast through car speakers
Hour 4: REST STOP — 15 min to run around
Hour 5: Surprise toys/gifts
Hour 6+: Screen time — deploy the tablets
Last stretch: Car games and sing-alongs to finish strong

Canada Border Crossing Guide

Everything you need for smooth crossings with kids

Required Documents — Keep Together & Accessible

🔒 Entering Canada (Ambassador Bridge)

  • Valid US Passport for each adult
  • Valid US Passport for each child OR
  • Original/certified birth certificate for kids under 16 (land crossing only)
  • Enhanced Driver's License (MI residents — accepted at land borders)
  • Vehicle registration
  • Proof of auto insurance
  • Notarized consent letter (if one parent absent)

🇺🇸 Returning to USA (Peace Bridge)

  • Same passport/ID documents as entry
  • Keep all Canadian purchase receipts
  • Declare all purchases at US customs
  • Duty-free allowance: $200 USD for trips 24-48 hrs
  • No fresh fruits/vegetables across border
  • No more than $10,000 currency undeclared

Traveling with Children — Special Notes

If Both Parents Are in the Car

You're all set as long as each person has valid ID. Passports for the whole family is the simplest approach. Border agents may ask your kids basic questions (their name, where they're going) — prep them ahead of time so they're not nervous.

If Only One Parent Is Driving

This is critical: Carry a notarized consent letter from the absent parent. The letter should include:

• The children's full names and dates of birth

• The traveling parent's full name

• The absent parent's full name and contact information

• Travel dates and destination

• A statement of permission: "I consent to my child(ren) traveling to Canada with [parent name]"

• Notarized signature of the absent parent

Canada takes child safety at borders very seriously. Without this letter, you may face significant delays or be denied entry.

Making the Crossing Smooth with Kids

• Before the booth: Turn off music, wake sleeping kids (agents may want to see them), have documents in hand

• Stack documents: All passports together in one hand, registration and insurance in the other

• Be friendly and concise: "Family road trip to Boston" is all you need

• Don't volunteer extra info: Answer questions directly without over-explaining

• Prep the 8-year-old: They may be asked questions directly — practice "What's your name?" and "Where are you going?"

• Have snacks ready: Unpredictable wait times are easier with fed kids

Crossing Logistics

Ambassador Bridge (Into Canada)

  • Toll: ~$5 USD (cash or card)
  • Open 24/7
  • Best times: Early morning, mid-week
  • Worst times: Friday 3-7 PM, holiday weekends
  • Average wait: 15-45 min
  • Check wait times: borderlineups.com

Peace Bridge (Back to USA)

  • Toll: ~$6 USD (cash or card)
  • Open 24/7
  • Generally less busy than Rainbow Bridge
  • Average wait: 15-30 min
  • Fort Erie, ON to Buffalo, NY
  • Check wait times: peacebridge.com
