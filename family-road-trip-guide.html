Family Road Trip Guide

Lawrence, MI → Boston, MA — 2 Days, 2 Kids

Day 1: Lawrence, MI to Buffalo/Amherst, NY (~9-10 hrs driving)

⛽
Costco Gas — Kalamazoo, MI
5100 Century Ave · First fill-up
~8:30 AM ▶

29 miles from Lawrence (~31 min). Fill up the tank and grab any last-minute snacks from the Costco warehouse before hitting I-94 East toward Detroit.

150 mi · ~2 hr 15 min via I-94 E
⛽
Costco Gas — Madison Heights, MI
30550 Stephenson Hwy · Head south to Ohio Turnpike
~11:00 AM ▶

Last stop in Michigan. Let the kids stretch their legs. From here, take I-75 S toward Toledo to pick up the Ohio Turnpike (I-80/I-90 E).

Michigan rest areas along I-75 feature playgrounds — perfect for burning off energy before the long Ohio stretch.
~185 mi · ~3 hrs via I-75 S / Ohio Turnpike (I-80/90 E)
⛽
Costco Gas — Mentor, OH
9250 Market Place · East of Cleveland on I-90
~2:30 PM ▶

Convenient fuel stop just off I-90 east of Cleveland. Good lunch break spot — plenty of restaurants in the Mentor area.

Optional Cleveland detour: The Great Lakes Science Center (downtown Cleveland, ~25 mi west) has hands-on exhibits perfect for both ages if you have extra time.
~175 mi · ~2 hr 45 min via I-90 E (through Erie, PA)
★
Optional: Presque Isle State Park — Erie, PA
Lake Erie beaches · Quick leg-stretch stop
Optional ▶

If the kids need a real break, Presque Isle is ~10 minutes off I-90 in Erie. Sandy Lake Erie beaches, easy walking trails, and usually much cooler than inside the car. Free entry.

Continue ~90 mi on I-90 E into New York
⛽
Costco Gas — Amherst, NY (Buffalo area)
4230 Ridge Lea Rd · Fill up for the night
~5:30 PM ▶

Gas up for the night. ⚠️ Verify this location is open before your trip (expected to open Spring 2026). If not yet open, Rochester’s Costco is ~70 mi further east.

☾
Overnight — Buffalo/Amherst Area
Recommended: Home2 Suites or Hampton Inn Amherst
Night ▶

Home2 Suites by Hilton Buffalo-Amherst — Suites with kitchenettes, pool, free breakfast. Great for families.

Hampton Inn Buffalo-Amherst — Free breakfast, pool, reliable Hilton standard.

Embassy Suites Buffalo — 2-room suites, cooked-to-order breakfast, indoor pool.

Dinner ideas: Anderson’s Frozen Custard (Buffalo classic), or the Walden Galleria mall area has tons of family restaurants. Kids will love a Buffalo chicken wing dinner the grown-ups can enjoy too!

Day 2: Buffalo, NY to Boston, MA (~7-8 hrs driving)

★
Optional Morning Stop — Buffalo Attractions
Before hitting the road
~9:00 AM ▶

If you're not in a rush, Buffalo has great family options before you leave:

• Niagara Falls State Park (US side) — ~30 mins north. Free park entry, Maid of the Mist boat ride (~$25 adults / $15 kids), incredible views from Prospect Point.

• Buffalo Zoo — Compact and walkable, great for young kids. One of the oldest zoos in the US.

• Explore & More Children’s Museum — Hands-on learning on the Buffalo waterfront.

If you visit Niagara Falls, park at Niagara Falls State Park on the US side — no border crossing needed, and the views are still spectacular.
69 mi · ~1 hr 10 min via I-90 E
★
Strong National Museum of Play — Rochester, NY
1 Manhattan Square Dr · World’s largest toy museum
Optional ▶

Incredible stop for both ages. Massive interactive exhibits, a working carousel, and a Sesame Street exhibit. Worth an hour even if you can’t do the whole museum.

If you skip this, the Seneca Park Zoo in Rochester is a smaller, quicker option with 90+ species and easy walks for little legs.
68 mi · ~1 hr 5 min via I-90 E
⛽
Costco Gas — Camillus, NY (Syracuse)
120 Township Blvd · Lunch break stop
~12:30 PM ▶

Gas up and grab lunch. Destiny USA mall is 10 min away with tons of family restaurants — and an indoor go-kart track and bowling if the kids need to burn off energy.

141 mi · ~2 hr 10 min via I-90 E
⛽
Costco Gas — Westmere/Albany, NY
Near Crossgates Mall · Last fill before Mass Pike
~3:00 PM ▶

Fill up here — it's ~180 miles to the next Costco in Dedham, MA. Let the kids stretch and use the restroom.

Crossgates Mall is right here if you need a real break — food court, play areas, and plenty of options.
~100 mi · ~1 hr 30 min via I-90 E
★
Springfield, MA — Optional Stops
I-90 corridor · Great for an afternoon break
~4:30 PM ▶

• The Amazing World of Dr. Seuss Museum — Interactive exhibits celebrating the beloved author. The 4-year-old will especially love this.

• Basketball Hall of Fame — Great for the 8-year-old if they're into sports. Interactive shooting courts.

• Six Flags New England (Agawam, just south) — If you have a full afternoon and want a big adventure.

~80 mi · ~1 hr 15 min via I-90 E
⛽
Costco Gas — Dedham, MA
200 Legacy Blvd · Final fill-up!
~6:30 PM ▶

Just southwest of Boston. Top off the tank and you're 25 minutes from downtown!

~12 mi · ~25 min via I-95 N
❤
Welcome to Boston!
You made it! Time to explore.
~7:00 PM ▶

Great first-afternoon activities with kids:

• Boston Public Garden — Ride the famous Swan Boats from "Make Way for Ducklings." Free to walk through, Swan Boats ~$4.50/person.

• New England Aquarium — 200,000-gallon tank, penguins, touch pools. Both ages will love it.

• Boston Children's Museum — 3 floors of hands-on learning with a dedicated soft play area for little ones.

The Aquarium and Children's Museum are both on the waterfront and walkable from each other — easy to combine.

Car Entertainment Guide

Keep the kids happy for ~17 hours of driving across 2 days

The Golden Rule: Rotate Every 30-45 Minutes

Pack a "surprise bin" accessible from the front seat. Swap activities every 30-45 minutes to keep things fresh. Novelty is more powerful than any single toy — even dollar store surprises wrapped in tissue paper can buy you an hour of peace.

Screen-Free Activities

Melissa & Doug Water Wow Pads

Ages 4 & 8

Reusable water-based painting pads — fill the pen with water, paint the pages, let them dry, and do it again. Zero mess. These are absolute road trip gold.

Magnetic Drawing Boards & Activities

Ages 4 & 8

Magna Doodle-style drawing boards or magnetic tangram puzzles. The 8-year-old might enjoy magnetic building tiles. Nothing rolls under seats!

Sticker Books & Window Clings

Age 4 Age 8

Reusable sticker scenes for the 4-year-old. The 8-year-old might prefer sticker-by-number books (more challenging). Window clings from the dollar store let them decorate their car window.

New Library Books

Ages 4 & 8

Check out a stack of new-to-them books from the library before the trip. The novelty factor makes these way more engaging than books they already own. Graphic novels and "I Spy" books are great for the 8-year-old.

Coloring & Activity Books

Age 4 Age 8

Chunky crayons and simple coloring books for the 4-year-old. The 8-year-old might enjoy crossword puzzles, word searches, or Mad Libs (Junior edition). Use a clipboard or lap desk for a stable surface.

Small Toys & Figurines

Ages 4 & 8

A small bag of action figures, toy cars, or Polly Pockets. Bonus: wrap a few in tissue paper as "surprise gifts" to open at scheduled intervals. Dollar store toys work perfectly for this.

Audio Entertainment

Audiobooks (Play Through Car Speakers)

Ages 4 & 8

Download from your library's Libby or Hoopla app — free with a library card! Great picks that work for both ages:

• The Magic Tree House series — perfect for the 8-year-old, engaging enough for the 4-year-old

• Winnie the Pooh — timeless, gentle, both ages love it

• Dog Man series — funny and engaging for the 8-year-old

• Frog and Toad — short chapters, sweet stories, great for the 4-year-old

Kid-Friendly Podcasts

Ages 4 & 8

Download episodes before the trip (cell service may be spotty in rural areas of Ohio and New York):

• Wow in the World — science and wonder, great for curious kids

• Story Pirates — stories written by kids, performed by comedians

• But Why? — answers kids' real questions about the world

• Circle Round — folktales from around the world

Music & Sing-Alongs

Ages 4 & 8

Create a family road trip playlist before you leave! Mix their favorites with road trip classics. Freeze dance (everyone freezes when you pause the music) works even in car seats.

Screen Time (Strategic Use)

Tablets — The Secret Weapon

Ages 4 & 8

Download content before the trip — you'll lose cell service in parts of rural Ohio, Pennsylvania, and New York.

• Movies: Download 3-4 movies each on their tablets. Save the favorites for the longest/hardest stretches.

• Apps: Khan Academy Kids (age 4), Toca Boca apps (both), PBS Kids Games (age 4), Minecraft (age 8)

• Key tip: Get volume-limiting headphones for each kid. Your sanity will thank you.

Pro tip: Save screen time as your "ace in the hole" for the hardest parts of the drive — late afternoon on Day 1, or the last 2 hours before Boston.

Car Games (No Supplies Needed)

Games the Whole Family Can Play

Ages 4 & 8

• I Spy — Classic. The 4-year-old can play with colors ("I spy something blue").

• License Plate Game — Spot plates from different states/provinces. The 8-year-old can keep a written tally.

• 20 Questions — Great for the 8-year-old. Simplify for the 4-year-old (animal, food, or toy?).

• Would You Rather? — Silly options keep everyone laughing. "Would you rather have spaghetti hair or pizza ears?"

• The Alphabet Game — Find letters A-Z on signs, license plates, and billboards in order.

• Scavenger Hunt — Make a simple picture list before the trip (red barn, cow, bridge, police car, etc.).

Suggested Activity Rotation Schedule

First hour: Excitement carries them — books, window watching, car games
Hour 2: Activity bin — Water Wow, stickers, coloring
Hour 3: Audiobook or podcast through car speakers
Hour 4: REST STOP — 15 min to run around
Hour 5: Surprise toys/gifts
Hour 6+: Screen time — deploy the tablets
Last stretch: Car games and sing-alongs to finish strong
